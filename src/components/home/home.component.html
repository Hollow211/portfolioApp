Edge detection using difference of gaussians (DoG)

Tags: Python, Numpy, CV2, PIP, Image processing, Stylized Rendering

Exploring difference of gaussians (DoG) as a low pass filter which can eliminate high frequencies that in the context of image processing corresponds to edges.

I experminted using Extended which is a better version of DoG that adds new variables to the equation and further stylize the image, However it didn't suit my taste as it suffered from alot of noise.

In order to avoid this noise we use Filtered DoG which uses the structure tensors of the image to get the eigenvectors which allows us to to know which direction the gradients are pointing.

Flow-based Bilaterial Filter

Tags: Python, Numpy, Cuda, Image processing, Image Effect


Bilaterial filter is one of the simplest filters and has a wide range of uses like denoising, tone management, enhancements.
But the most important feature of the filter is its ability to smooth images while also perserving edges or discontinuities, this allows it to be one of the best filters when it comes to image abstraction in image processing term or image features reduction in deep/machine learning term.

While taking performance and the ability to produce quality images into account the Bilterial filter scores low as it's not separable (at least not without leaving many artifacts), also a Bileterial filter that convolves over all of the image will mostly just blur it instead of smoothing and keeping the discontinuities.

That's why we have to take local orientation of the image (Structure tensor) into account, we simply filter along along the local gauge coordinates of the image, instead of along the coordinate axes. In case of an edge, filtering along the gradient direction results in strengthening of the edge, while filtering along the tangent direction results in a smoothing operation along the edge, avoiding artifacts and producing more coherent region boundaries.



An example of applying the filter reducing the image features




Since we now have a limited palette and a very abstracted image, we can apply pixelization filter on the result to get a nicely pixelated image of different resolutions.

By simply scaling our abstracted image, we get some really nice pixel art look.
